FAQ
8

What is HackExeter?

Code 11 hours nonstop. Make something awesome. Win cool prizes.

HackExeter is a faster-paced, themed, day of coding aimed at middle-schoolers and high-schoolers.

Am I good enough?

Yes! Our mentors are here to help you, even if you're new to programming.

What can I make?

Technology! Within that anything goes. If you are doing hardware stuff, though, bring your own tools.

Why a theme?

The theme is meant to help you get started. In an 11-hour hackathon there's no time to waste! Feel free to (mis)interpret our theme however you like.

We were inspired by js13k and the github game-off.

Only 11 hours?

HackExeter is a faster-paced contest than traditional hackathons. This makes the contest day more intense and panicky -- and fun! It also means that it's not a huge time or money commitment, so you can come.

How do we win awards?

We hope that you're coming less for the awards and more for the spirit of working together in teams to create and enjoy projects. That said, there will be awards for the top three projects with the highest overall scores. There will also be separate special award categories such as for "best abuse of a library" or "most comedic". You can view our Awards page here.

I can't really code.

Great! Come learn. There will be plenty of mentors floating around the room, ready to assist you if you have any inquiries or are in need of help. They will guide you through the process of bringing your idea to life. It'll be fun.

How are we judged?

You will do a live demo for us after the hacking period. We judge on creativity, novelty, technical difficulty, and theme relevance.

What can I bring?

A laptop and corresponding power cable is recommended. If you want to bring something else that you think would help in your project, go ahead! Anything legal and inanimate goes. We will provide breakfast, lunch, snacks, and dinner free of charge.

We will provide breakfast, lunch, snacks, and dinner free of charge.

Do I need a team?

Nope! You can join a pick-up team. Teams consist of four or five people. We would really like to pair individual attendees together to foster a spirit of collaboration and teamwork.

Is there a limit on students per school?

Nope! Teams are limited to 5 people, but a school can send as many teams as they like.

Register

Participation is free.
