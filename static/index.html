FAQ
8

What is HackExeter?

Code 11 hours nonstop. Make something awesome. Show it to judges. Win cool prizes.

Our hackathon is themed. We will announce the theme when we start the clock the day of.

Am I good enough?

Yes! Anyone passionate can compete. It's a competition as much about learning as it is about creating.

How are we judged?

You will demo your hack to us for scoring. We judge on creativity, novelty, difficulty, and relevance to our theme.

What can I make?

Most everything! It must be technology, made on-site, demoable, and somehow related to our theme. Not necessarily software, but bring your own tools.

Can I use prior work?

Feel free to use as much prior work as you want. You can even work on an ongoing project. However, you will only be judged on work done on-site.

Why a theme?

The theme is meant to help you get started. In an 11-hour hackathon there's no time to waste! Feel free to (mis)interpret our theme however you like.

We were inspired by js13k and the github game-off.

Only 11 hours?

Traditional hackathons are 24 hours or more. We wanted to do something more intense, more panicked, and more hacky -- to capture the "sports event" feel.

Hopefully, this will also be more accommodating for middle-school schedules.

What can I bring?

You should bring a laptop and charger. You can bring anything you want, so long as it is legal and not alive.

We will provide breakfast, lunch, snacks, and dinner free of charge.

Do I need a team?

Hacking is done in teams of four or five. You can also come alone and join a pick-up team.
